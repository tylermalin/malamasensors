01 · Company

What Mālama Labs builds

Mālama Labs is a verified environmental data company. We manufacture sensor hardware that signs every reading cryptographically at the device level, then anchors that proof permanently to a public blockchain. The result: a tamper-evident, continuous record of what happened in the physical world — tied to GPS coordinates, UTC timestamps, and a unique hardware identity that cannot be spoofed in software.

The climate credit market has a 30-year data integrity problem. Carbon offsets worth hundreds of billions of dollars are routinely verified by an auditor visiting a site once a year. We replace that with continuous, hardware-attested, on-chain proof. The device is the product. Everything else — the token, the credit instrument, the compliance disclosure — is an output of the device doing its job correctly.

The core trust mechanism sits in a single $1.25 chip: the ATECC608B secure element, which generates and stores an ECDSA P-256 private key at manufacture and signs every sensor reading before it leaves the device. That signature is verifiable by anyone, anywhere, forever.

02 · Network model

What DePIN means for this hardware

DePIN — Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network — is the model used by Helium (wireless), Hivemapper (mapping), and DIMO (vehicle data). The hardware manufacturer designs and ships the device. Operators buy and deploy it at their own cost. The network pays operators in a native token for verified uptime and data contribution.

Mālama's network token is MLMA (1 billion fixed supply, Cardano-native). Each Genesis 200 operator receives 125,000 MLMA with their node — 25% at mainnet launch, 75% vesting linearly over 12 months. The network grows because operators are economically incentivized to grow it.

The critical difference from Helium: our nodes produce data with direct regulatory and market value. Every signed reading is potential evidence toward a carbon credit, a compliance disclosure, or a climate prediction market settlement. The data is not coverage for coverage's sake. It is scientific record with a monetary output.

USE · 01
Proof of life / network bootstrap
200 deployed nodes across diverse climate zones establishes that the network is real, the hardware works, and the on-chain record is accumulating. Every SaveCard minted is a verifiable proof of physical operation. This is the foundation all future commercial activity sits on.
USE · 02
Early infrastructure support
Genesis nodes provide the baseline environmental data layer — soil moisture, atmospheric conditions, GPS-stamped readings — that subsequent professional sensor SKUs (ERW, Biochar, DAC) build on. Upgrading a hex zone to commercial instrumentation is a hardware swap, not an architecture change.
USE · 03
Prediction market settlement
Climate prediction markets on Kalshi and Polymarket require trusted data resolution. A network of 200 hardware-signed nodes producing continuous soil and atmospheric readings provides oracle-grade inputs for contracts on drought indices, soil moisture anomalies, and precipitation events — data sources that cannot be gamed because they originate in hardware.
USE · 04
Anti-spoofing AI integration
Non-oraclized data sources — satellite imagery, weather APIs, remote sensing — can be correlated against hardware-signed ground-truth readings to detect anomalies and flag potential spoofing or sensor drift. The Genesis network becomes the calibration layer for AI-driven environmental monitoring, project planning, and verification workflows.

04 · Forward roadmap

Professional sensor family — Genesis is the seed

The Genesis 300 establishes the hardware architecture, signing standard, and operator network. Every future professional SKU uses the same ATECC608B + Cardano anchoring + Mālama Cloud pipeline. Only the sensor layer changes. An operator upgrading a Genesis hex zone to commercial ERW instrumentation swaps the sensor module — not the trust architecture.
